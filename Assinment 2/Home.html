Muniba Mazari

Muniba Mazari Baloch is a Pakistani activist, anchor, artist, model, singer, and motivational speaker. She became the National Ambassador for UN Women Pakistan in 2015 and was listed in Forbes 30 under 30 in 2016.

Personal Life

Muniba Mazari was born in Rahim Yar Khan, Punjab, Pakistan. She belongs to the Baloch Mazari tribe and attended the Army Public School before pursuing a BFA. At 18, she was married, and in 2008, she was involved in a life-changing car accident that left her paraplegic.

Crash and Recovery

On 27 February 2008, while traveling from Quetta to Rahim Yar Khan, Muniba Mazari suffered severe injuries, including spinal damage that left her wheelchair-bound. After surgeries and two years of bed rest, she gradually recovered through physiotherapy.

Career

Muniba Mazari built a successful career as an artist and motivational speaker. She has worked with PTV, Clown Town, and other organizations. She was also the first wheelchair-using model in Asia for Toni & Guy’s Women of Substance campaign.

Art and Exhibitions

She started painting while bedridden and later launched her brand, Muniba’s Canvas. Her exhibitions have been held in Lahore, Dubai, and other locations, often supporting charity causes.

Awards and Recognition

  • 100 Inspirational Women of 2015 (BBC)
  • 500 Most Influential Muslims of the World
  • First Pakistani UN Goodwill Ambassador for UN Women
  • Forbes 30 Under 30 (2016)
  • The Karic Brothers Award (2017, Serbia)
